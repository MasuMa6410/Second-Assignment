Happiness (series)

This 12 episode series is the first that brilliantly takes elements of the pandemic to create a thriller that feels totally relatable. Because of what everyone has been through this past 2 years many elements of this show don't feel far fetched but instead are gripping and real. One of the best things about the show is it doesn't rely on a lot of gore or shock tactics or a monster a minute kind of thing...

Red Notice 1 (movie)

I all honesty, the film isn't that bad - it's just something that's been so over-hyped from marketing it was a disappointment to watch. The film has smashed Netflix records for viewership but I think that's thanks to the marketing teams of all three major stars and the limitless budgets from Netflix. The film is very generic and follows a format most viewers are familiar with yet are told "it's something never been done before" and "totally unexpected" etc. I can't imagine this is a film you would want to sit down to watch once or twice a year but more something that'll be played on ITV2 at Christmas or a Bank Holiday.
